Who Am I?

My name is Evan Barosay, it’s nice to meet you. I am a senior at the University of California, San Diego studying Human Computer Interaction & Design. I study design-related technology with a focus on user-interaction. I will be graduating June 2018. I'm interested in designing systems and products that have an emphasis on producing civic good— which I believe can be attained in any field, business, or product-service.


My Path

Design wasn’t always my pursued path. I originally entered UCSD as a Human Biology major, on the pre-med track. However, after determining that medicine was not a strong interest of mine, I took a quarter to explore different majors and classes that were offered. Luckily, I took an Intro to Human-Centered Design course, and immediately knew I was drawn to the field of design. Since then, design classes have become a true enjoyment, and have prompted me to pursue further design experience as Research Assistants in UCSD’s Design Lab and the UCSD Cognitive Science Department. Through this, I have gained insight in the field of human-centered design and have also gained skills revolving around human-behavior and decision making, prototyping and iterating, user-research and evidence-based design, and (perhaps the most important) team-working and team-building. I have far more to learn in this field and am eager to do so. I am excited for the future, whatever and wherever that might be.


My Approach

As a current HCI student, using research-driven design to create and accommodate different narratives that also complement the overall human-centered design has become one of the most fundamental parts in my design process. I like to follow an approach of observational-research, aberrant ideation, multi-leveled prototyping, and iteration. I believe good design starts with ample research to find the root problem(s), and is driven by unconventional solutions, all while keeping the intended user in mind.
